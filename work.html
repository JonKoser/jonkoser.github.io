Wildland Urban Interface - Minnesota

This is one of the spreads I helped produce throught he UW-Cartography Lab for the National Forest Service. It is one of 49 such spreads, one or each conterminous US state plus Washington D.C, created for an atlas showing the Wildland Urban Interface for forest fires. This was produced with Adobe Illustrator and Adobe InDesign.

Old Salt Route

This is my map of the Old Salt Route in northern Germany. This map won the CaGIS Arthur Robinson Award in cartography as well as the Barbar Petchenik award for cartographic design in 2014 and is now archived in the Library of Congress. This was made using ArcGIS and edited in Adobe Illustrator.

Maori Census

This is a website I built using D3.js which takes census data from the New Zealand census and focuses on Maori statistics. Unlike most census maps put out by the New Zealand census, this interactive visualzation aggregates to the smaller district level as oppesed to the larger province level.

Europe's Historic Population

This is a website I built using Leaflet.js to show historic population trends throughout Europe from 1300 to the present day. It follows the top 50 most populated cities in each era.

Go to Website

The Frozen Frontier

This is a website built using D3.js by myself, Roar Engle, and Brandon Kutsch. It maps out and plots on a timeline important historic events surrounding soverignty in the Arctic circle since 1903. Since the Arctic is recently becoming a hot topic in politics again, it was our intention to show the historical precedent for current hostilities and moves.

Go to Website

The Russian Railroad in 1914

This map shows the railroad network in European Russia in 1914. This was done as part of an article published by a history professor at the University of Wisconsin - Madison, which is why it highlights specific provinces (gubernii) and cities. It was made made by carefully digitizing historical maps in QGIS and exporting to PDF to be edited in Illustrator with Avenza MAPublisher.

Full Sized Image

Nam Theun II Dam

I made this map for Professor Ian Baird for his journal artical about the Nam Theun 2 dam in Laos and its connection to the Thai power grid. The article, along with this map, is published in the Annals of the Association of American Geographers. This was created using Avenza MAPublisher in Adobe Illustrator.
